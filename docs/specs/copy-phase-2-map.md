# Phase 2 Copy Map

This phase covers the shared informational pages.

## Source Rules

- Use historic copy verbatim where it is strong, specific, and still accurate.
- Use current-site facts for contact, rental cadence, and cottage basics.
- Remove all language that explains the rebuild or the website itself.
- Give each page one job and avoid repeating the same property summary everywhere.

## Page Ownership

- About:
  - Family continuity, setting, and the long-running character of the camps.
- Directions:
  - Direct route guidance and simple location context only.
- Photos:
  - Explain that current photographs show the cottages as they are now.
- Contact:
  - Tell people how to inquire and what details to include.
- Legacy:
  - Explain the archive as older photographs of the camps and surrounding place.

## About

- Current:
  - Still paraphrases where the historic site already has stronger language.
- Historic source:
  - "Cook's By The Ocean has been managing this cottage resort since 1932."
  - "We are on our fifth generation of families returning year after year."
  - "The cottages are scattered about on the sand dunes 125 feet above the beach on the Atlantic Ocean side of Cape Cod."
  - "This is a very special place, and it is our wonderful guests that visit year after year that make it so special."
- Direction:
  - Use the historic text directly, trimming only where the facts no longer match the current site.
  - Avoid adding explanatory framing around the historic lines.
